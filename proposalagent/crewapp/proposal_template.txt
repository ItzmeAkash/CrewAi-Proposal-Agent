U.S. DEPARTMENT OF STATE U.S. Embassy Ethiopia Public Diplomacy Section Notice of Funding Opportunity: Statement of Interest

Funding Opportunity Title: U.S. Embassy Ethiopia PD Request for Statement of Interest
Funding Opportunity Number: PDS-004-FY2024
Second Round - Date Opened: April 02, 2024
Second Round - Deadline for Applications: April 30, 2024
CFDA Number: 19.040 – Public Diplomacy Programs (Smith-Mundt)
Total Amount Available: Approximately $200,000, pending funding availability.
Award amounts: Awards may range from a minimum of $25,000 to a maximum of $100,000. Exceptional proposals above $200,000 may be considered pending funding availability.
Federal Agency Email: AddisPASAPSGrants@state.gov

A. PROGRAM DESCRIPTION
The U.S. Embassy Ethiopia Public Diplomacy (PD) section of the U.S. Department of State announces an open competition for organizations or individuals to submit a statement of interest (SOI) to carry out a public engagement program. Please carefully review the following information to ensure your proposal aligns with the funding opportunity.

B. ELIGIBILITY
Eligible applicants include individuals, non-governmental organizations, think tanks, and academic institutions. The following types of programs are not eligible for funding:
- Programs relating to partisan political activity
- Charitable or development activities
- Construction programs
- Programs that support specific religious activities
- Fundraising campaigns
- Lobbying for specific legislation or programs
- Scientific research
- Programs intended primarily for the growth or institutional development of the organization; or
- Programs that duplicate existing programs.

C. THEMATIC AREAS
This Notice of Funding Opportunity (NOFO) aims to support projects in at least one of the following thematic areas:
- Efforts to support Ethiopia’s economic and political reforms
- Support for civil society, democracy, and governance

D. APPLICATION PROCESS
All application materials must be submitted by email to AddisPASAPSGrants@state.gov. Please ensure that your submission adheres to the guidelines outlined in the NOFO.

E. APPLICATION REVIEW INFORMATION
1. Criteria
Each SOI will be evaluated and rated based on the following criteria:
Quality of Program Idea – 50 points: The SOI is responsive to the program framework. The idea is communicated effectively and is feasible. Proposals that introduce creative approaches to address program objectives are highly encouraged. The proposed timeline and budget should be reasonable. Embassy priority areas and target audiences must be clearly addressed. Proposals should also demonstrate how the program advances equity with respect to race, gender, and other underserved communities.

2. Submission Deadline
The deadline for submitting SOIs is April 30, 2024. Late submissions will not be considered.

For any questions or clarifications regarding the funding opportunity, please contact AddisPASAPSGrants@state.gov. We look forward to receiving your innovative proposals that contribute to the advancement of public diplomacy in Ethiopia.
